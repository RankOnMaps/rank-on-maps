01 · What is the CRM

It's your business in one screen.

Think of the CRM as the central nervous system of Builders Collective. Every lead, every call, every text, every appointment — it all lives in one place.

In one line
A CRM (Customer Relationship Management system) is how you keep track of every person who's ever shown interest in building with you — from the first inquiry all the way to handover.

You're set up on CloseBetter CRM — that's your branded version. Behind the scenes it's powered by enterprise-grade software (the same kind big agencies use), but the interface is clean and made for you.

What it replaces

×
Before

Spreadsheets. Sticky notes. "Did Hasib follow up with that REA lead?" Phone calls to find out who's doing what. Leads going cold.

✓
After

One screen shows every lead, who owns it, where it is in the process, what's been said, what's coming next. From your phone or laptop.

02 · How leads flow through it

Five places leads come in.

Every lead that hits your business comes through one of five channels. The CRM catches all of them automatically.

1
REA (realestate.com.au)
Someone inquires on one of your 50 listings. Lands in CRM with the suburb, package and price already attached.
2
Meta Ads (Facebook + Instagram)
From the ads Kristalyn runs. Lead form fill → CRM → instant follow-up.
3
Google Ads
High-intent searches like "home builders Perth". They're already comparison shopping.
4
Website + Live Chat
Forms on builderscollective.au, plus the chat widget. Warmest leads — they're already on your site.
5
Phone (incl. AI after-hours)
Inbound calls. After 8pm the AI voice agent takes the call, captures their details, and books them in.
Important
No matter which channel a lead comes from, they all land in the same CRM. The system tags each one (e.g. "source:rea") so you always know where they came from and which ad spent the money to get them there.
03 · Your day-to-day

What you'll actually do.

Most of the work is automated. Your job, day-to-day, is straightforward.

1
Check the app each morning

Open the CloseBetter mobile app. See your new leads, your booked calls today, and any follow-ups due.

2
Take your calls

Leads book themselves into your calendar. You just show up. The CRM has already sent them context emails.

3
Update the deal after each call

Move the card to the next pipeline stage. Add a couple of notes. The next email goes out automatically.

4
Reply when someone replies

If a lead replies to an automated email, the system pauses the automation and tags you to handle it personally.

That's it. Most days you'll spend less time on admin than you do now, even though you'll have way more leads moving through.

06 · When you take a call

The 4-step call process.

Same simple process every time. Don't overthink it.

1
Before the call (2 minutes)
Open the contact card in CloseBetter. Read the notes the system has captured (where they came from, anything they typed into the form, any prior messages). Be ready.
2
During the call (15 minutes)
Listen first. Understand their situation. Ask about block status, budget, timeline, finance. Mention the vetting angle. Take quick notes in the contact's "Notes From Call" field.
3
Right after the call (5 minutes)
Tag them: Audience Type (FHB / Upgrader / Investor) and Timeline (Hot / Warm / Cold). Move the pipeline card. Trigger the post-call recap email (you'll fill in 3 bullet points — system sends).
4
Within 24 hours
If they're qualified, send the builder shortlist or schedule the next step. If they're not yet ready, the system will check back in for you. Either way, your work is done.
07 · 5 rules to live by

Stick to these, and the system works.

Most CRMs fail because the team stops using them. Five rules to keep that from happening here.

1
Always update the pipeline stage

After every meaningful interaction, drag the deal to the right stage. If you don't, the wrong emails go out and the dashboard lies.

2
Tag every lead within 24 hours of first contact

Source tag is automatic. You add: Audience Type + Timeline. Takes 5 seconds, makes the segmentation work.

3
Reply to replies fast

When a lead replies, the system pauses automation and pings you. You've got 60 minutes. Don't let it sit.

4
Never delete a contact

If they're not buying, move them to "Not Yet Ready" or "Lost". Don't delete. We track everything for future re-engagement.

5
Check the dashboard weekly

Every Monday, look at where leads are stalling and where the spend is converting. Adjust accordingly. → Open dashboard

08 · Glossary

The words you'll hear.

CRM
Customer Relationship Management — the software that tracks every lead, every conversation, every deal. Yours is CloseBetter.
Pipeline
The visual board showing every deal and what stage it's in. Drag cards across as deals progress.
Lead source
Where a lead came from (REA / Meta / Google / Website / Phone). Auto-tagged by the system.
Discovery call
The 15-minute call you take to qualify a new lead. Round-robin distributed across the 4 of you.
PPA
Preliminary Property Agreement — the contract a buyer signs with a builder before the final HIA contract. Includes a small deposit (typically $1-3K). This is when your commission becomes locked in.
Building Contract (HIA)
The full, formal building contract. Signed after the PPA and after plans are finalised. Triggers the inspector workflow.
Workflow
An automated sequence of emails, SMS, and tasks triggered by a specific event (e.g. a booking, a stage move).
Nurture
The multi-day email + SMS sequence that runs for leads who haven't booked yet. 21 days, then they go to long-term follow-up.
Round-robin
How leads are distributed across the team — automatically rotated so everyone gets an equal share.
VSL
Video Sales Letter — the 2-3 minute explainer video on the landing page. Pre-sells before the call.
Inspector
The independent third-party building inspector who does random site visits during your clients' builds. Their fee is built into your package.
AI voice agent
Answers calls between 8pm and 6am. Takes details, books calls. Doesn't replace you — it just makes sure leads aren't lost overnight.
