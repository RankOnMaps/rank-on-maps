Before we call

3 things to think about. Takes 5 minutes.

You don't need to have everything figured out. But the more clarity you bring to the call, the more we can pack into the 15 minutes.

01
Where you want to build
Got a block already? Awesome — have the address handy. Still looking? List 2-3 suburbs you're considering and your max travel time to work.
02
Your budget honestly
No need for an exact number — a range works (e.g. "$400-500K all-in"). If you've spoken to a broker or bank, jot down your pre-approval. If not, we'll point you at someone good.
03
Your non-negotiables
Double garage? Granny flat capability? Solar-ready? Open plan? Two storeys? Write down 3-5 must-haves so we don't waste time on builders who can't deliver them.

Before you hop on

Common questions. Quick answers.

Do I have to pay anything for this call?
No. The call is free, and there's no fee to use Builders Collective. We're paid by the builder when we successfully match a client — never by you. You get an independent advocate at zero cost.
What if I've already spoken to a builder?
Even better. Bring their quote and we'll tear through it for free — we'll tell you exactly what's missing, what's overpriced, and whether you're getting a fair deal. Most clients save $15-30K just from getting a second opinion.
What if I don't have a block yet?
Perfectly fine. About 40% of our clients come to us before buying land. We'll help you understand what's available, what to look for, and which suburbs match your build vision and budget.
How long until I can move in?
Typically 12 months for a new home from contract sign, 8 months for a granny flat. Faster if your block is ready, slower if you need approvals. We'll give you a realistic timeline on the call.
What if I'm not ready for another 6 months?
Still take the call. The earlier you understand your options, grants, and budget, the better positioned you'll be when you ARE ready. We can also keep you updated as new builder offers come up.
Can I bring my partner?
Please do. Building decisions almost always involve two people — we'd rather have both of you on the line than redo the conversation twice. Just let us know when we call.
